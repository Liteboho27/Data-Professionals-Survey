# Data-Professional-Survey

### 📋 Project Overview

This interactive Power BI dashboard analyses the 2022 Data Professional Survey dataset collected by Alex The Analyst (630 respondents). The report explores key aspects of the data industry, including salary trends by job title, preferred programming languages across different data roles, career satisfaction levels (salary, work/life balance, skills development and upward mobility) and insights into breaking into data careers.

Through extensive data cleaning in Power Query (including splitting multi-value fields like favorite programming languages), DAX calculations, and thoughtful visualisations, the dashboard delivers clear, actionable insights into compensation, skills, and job satisfaction in the data field. As an aspiring Data Analyst passionate about turning raw data into meaningful business stories, this project allowed me to strengthen my skills in end-to-end analytics, from data transformation and modeling to building interactive, user-friendly dashboards that drive better decision-making.

## 🎯 Key Insights

- Data Scientists and Data Engineers have significantly higher salaries compared to Data Analysts.
- Python dominates across all data professions, followed closely by other languages/tools (including SQL)
- Most repondents (42.7%) find it neither difficult nor easy to break into the data profession, but a significant number (24.7%) consider it difficult to break into the profession. The most prominent roles to break into are Data Scientist and Data Architect.
- Better Salary, Remote work and Good work/life balance are the **top 3** most important factors that people conmsider when applying for data roles

## 📊 Dashboard Highlights

**Salary Analysis** 
- **Data Scientists, Data Architects, and Data Engineers** rank as the **top 3 highest-paid roles**, significantly outpacing other positions.
- **Data Analysts** rank 5th in average salary, highlighting a clear compensation gap between advanced analytical/engineering roles and traditional analyst positions, a key consideration for career planning in the data field.

**Skills & Tools** 
- **Python** emerges as the dominant programming language, preferred by the majority of data professionals across multiple roles. This underscores Python’s critical importance for anyone entering or advancing in the data industry.
- The **Skills Development** happiness score stands at **5.61** (above average), indicating strong overall satisfaction with learning opportunities and continuous growth in data roles.

**Satisfaction Metrics**
- Professionals report the **highest happiness** in **Work/Life Balance (5.74)**, while **Salary Satisfaction** lags significantly at **4.27** and **Upward Mobility** at **4.76**.
- This gap reveals that while data roles offer good lifestyle balance, compensation and career progression remain pain points that organizations may need to address to improve retention.

**Demographics**
- **Female respondents** account for a slight majority of earnings share (**51.37%**) compared to males (**48.63%**), suggesting improving gender representation in compensation within the surveyed data community.

**Career Entry** 
- The **top three industries** attracting career switchers into data roles are **Technology, Finance, and Healthcare**.
- This highlights strong demand and transferable opportunities for professionals pivoting into data from these sectors.
- **Python** is the must-have skill for data professionals. Strong proficiency can significantly boost employability across roles.
- Specialised roles (Data Scientist, Data Engineer, Data Architect) command substantially higher salaries than traditional Data Analyst positions.
- While work/life balance is a strong point, **salary satisfaction** and **upward mobility** remain major pain points that could impact talent retention.
- The data industry continues to attract career switchers, especially from **Tech, Finance, and Healthcare**, creating clear pathways for new talent.

**Overall Insight**
This dashboard highlights both the opportunities and challenges in the data job market, helping organisations improve compensation strategies and professionals make smarter career decisions.
